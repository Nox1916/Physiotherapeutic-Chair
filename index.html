Physiotherapeutic Multi Exercise Chair

The main objective of the development of a Multi-Exercise Physiotherapeutic Chair is to create a creative and reasonably priced physiotherapy chair specifically designed for people suffering from immobility due to Arthritis or frozen shoulder. By means of an affordable and user-friendly intervention, this research aims to increase physical activity, promote quality of life, and preserve independence in these populations, who frequently face serious mobility issues as a result of age and disease development. A variety of features designed to meet the needs of people with restricted mobility are included into the physiotherapy chair. Ergonomic design to guarantee comfort and safety, and automated and customizable systems for both passive and active exercises. The chair will facilitate several therapeutic exercises for the arms, legs therefore encouraging general physical well-being. It will also provide configurable settings to suit different therapy needs and stages of advancement.

About Physio Chair

Welcome to our project dedicated to improving the lives of individuals with limited mobility due to Arthritis or frozen shoulder. Our main objective is to develop a Multi-Exercise Physiotherapeutic Chair that is both creative and reasonably priced, specifically designed to address the unique needs of this population.

We envision a world where everyone, regardless of age or mobility limitations, can access effective physiotherapy solutions. By developing the Multi-Exercise Physiotherapeutic Chair, we strive to empower individuals to maintain their independence, enhance their quality of life, and engage in physical activities that are crucial for their health and well-being.

Our physiotherapy chair incorporates a variety of features tailored to meet the needs of people with restricted mobility:

  • Ergonomic Design: Ensuring comfort and safety, the chair is designed with user-friendly ergonomics in mind.
  • Automated and Customizable Systems: Equipped for both passive and active exercises, our chair allows users to engage in therapeutic movements at their own pace and ability level.
  • Comprehensive Exercise Support: The chair supports a range of therapeutic exercises for the arms and legs, promoting overall physical well-being.

Our mission is to create an affordable, user-friendly intervention that increases physical activity, promotes quality of life, and preserves independence for those who often face severe mobility challenges due to age and disease progression. We aim to provide a solution that is accessible to everyone in need, ensuring that cost and complexity are no longer barriers to effective physiotherapy.

Theoretical Design

The design of a theoretical CAD model involves several steps: object identification, requirement collection, conceptualization, and software selection. We chose SolidWorks for this process. The design begins with creating a digital model, checking for dimensional constraints, and refining through iterations. The design is validated, recorded, and reviewed before finalization. The model is then exported and revision control is applied for future iterations. Prototyping and testing confirm the design's success, emphasizing the importance of a comprehensive design process beyond theoretical development.

Production Drawings

Production drawings are essential for manufacturing of any product such as chair as they provide precise dimensions, materials, and finishes for each part, ensuring accuracy and consistency. These act as a universal language among designers, engineers, and manufacturers, clearly conveying all necessary information. These drawings include tolerances for quality control and detailed assembly instructions, streamlining the production process and reducing errors. By maintaining uniform standards, production drawings ensure each chair is produced consistently and efficiently, meeting design specifications and quality requirements.

Manufacturing Plan

The team collaborated to devise the most economical and effective way to prototype the "Multi-Exercise Physiotherapeutic Chair". After the Design Phase, we conducted a market investigation to identify vendors and estimate component costs, exploring local and online stores to find affordable, in-stock parts.

Using CAD software, we developed a detailed digital model of the chair, assessing ergonomics, structural integrity, and assembly feasibility to optimize the design for manufacturing and functionality. This led to a thorough inventory of required components, covering construction materials, seat elements, and flexible mechanisms.

We then performed a pricing comparison to find cost-effective suppliers, considering unit prices, volume discounts, shipping costs, and delivery times to stay within budget. An analysis determined which components to purchase and which to produce in-house, factoring in technical skills, resource availability, time constraints, and budget to ensure project success.
